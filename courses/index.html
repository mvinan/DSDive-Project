DISCOVERY SCUBA DIVING

If you want to find out but are not quite ready to jump into a certification course, Discover Scuba Diving Scuba divers or program will let you try scuba to see if you like.

While not an actual scuba certification, during the Discover Scuba Diving experience or baptism dive you will learn how to use scuba equipment in shallow water and get a quick and easy introduction to what it takes to explore the underwater world.

The fun part

There is nothing like breathing underwater for the first time.

It takes some time to adjust, after all, humans are not made to do this though after a few minutes of breathtaking wonder, most participants realize how easy it really is diving.

The biggest challenge instructors face is not encouraging participants to go overboard, but persuade them to leave when the program has finished.

The fun part? - Everything is fun!

What you will learn ?

You will learn the basics you need to dive under the direct supervision of a PADI professional. One thing you'll learn is that you can actually breathe underwater.

- Learn how you feel diving equipment set and how easy it is to move in the water while you wear

- Realize what is to breathe underwater and discover if you can find comfortable there.

- Learn some basic exercises and safety rules that take you to your full certification course when you take the next step

Diving equipment you will use

All the equipment you'll need is typically included in a Discover Scuba Diving experience or baptism dive.

You'll use all the basic scuba gear including a mask, snorkel and fins, a compensator vest buoyancy also support your scuba tank, regulator diving breathing through it and instrumentation to monitor depth and supplies breathable gas.

Also you will take a damp wetsuit, a ballast system depending on where you make the Discover Scuba Diving program.

The Learning Materials You'll Need

Only your disposal and your full attention.

Prerequisites

At least 10 years of age.

RESCUE DIVER

"Challenging" and rewarding eis what best describes the PADI Rescue Diver. Based on what you've already learned, this course expands on the knowledge you already have about how to prevent problems and how to manage them if they occur

The fun part

The fun part about this course is to rise above the challenges and master them. Most divers say this course is demanding and rewarding, and at the end say it is the best course I have ever taken.

Get credits

You can get earn college credit for the PADI Rescue Diver.

What you will learn

Auto bailout.

Recognizing and managing stress in other divers.

Equipment and emergency management.

Rescue divers panic.

Rescue divers unconscious

Diving equipment you will use

You will use all your basic scuba gear including a dive computer and accessories.
Check with your local dive center on the equipment you will use during this course. You can find most everything at the dive shop in your area.

ADVANCE OPEN WATER DIVER

The Advanced Open Water Diver course helps you increase your confidence and build your scuba skills so you can feel more comfortable in the water

It's a great way to get more dives under your Blog while continuing to learn under the supervision of your instructor BuceoTortuga your school Divers. This course consolidates what you've learned and develops new capabilities by introducing new activities and new ways to have fun scuba diving. You hone your skills doing four dives adventure, namely

- Underwater Navigation

- Deep Diver (usually anywhere between 18-30 meters / 60-100 feet)

- A sample three Adventure Dives of your choice

The fun part

One of the reasons why you like the Advanced Open Water Diver is that together with your instructor Darwin Dive Scuba Divers, you will make a choice from among 15 types of Adventure Dives to complete the course. You might want to try digital underwater photography, night diving, scuba diver propulsion vehicle, peak performance buoyancy, underwater navigation, and more.

What you will learn

The knowledge and skills you will acquire in the Advanced Open Water Diver vary the interest you have and the adventures you live, but include:

- Practical aspects of deep diving,

- Physiological effects of deep diving.

- Underwater Navigation and more ways to use your compass.

- Buoyancy

- To navigate using fin strokes, visual references and time.

- How to better use your dive computer and Dive Planner recreational

- And much, much more, depending on the Adventure Dives you choose.

Diving equipment you will use

Use all basic diving equipment including underwater compass, a dive, balloons lifting a banner / buoy diving and other specialized equipment.

DIVE MASTER

Divemaster training develops your leadership abilities, qualifying you to supervise dive activities and assist instructors with student divers. The Divemaster is the prerequisite for certification PADI Assistant Instructor and PADI Open Water Scuba Instructor.

What you will learn

During the PADI Divemaster program, you learn dive leadership skills through both classroom study and independently. You complete water and stamina exercises, as well as training exercises that stretch your ability to organize and solve problems and to help others improve their scuba.
You put this knowledge through a structured integration period or series of practical training exercises.

What You Can Teach

Once certified as a PADI Divemaster I these authorized:

- Supervise activities both training and non training through planning, organizing and directing dives.

- Assist a PADI Instructor during the training sessions of any PADI dive.

- Conduct the PADI Skin Diver and PADI Discover Snorkeling program.

- Conduct the PADI Discover Local Diving experience.

- Conduct the PADI Scuba Review program.

- If qualified as a Discover Scuba Diving Leader, directing regardless the PADI Discover Scuba Diving program.

- Get the PADI Digital Underwater Photographer Specialty Instructor to teach the PADI Digital Underwater Photographer specialty

- Students Independently guide Open Water Diver course in the part of Ride Open Water Diver course Training Dives for 2, 3 and 4 in a ratio of two student divers per certified divemaster.


Accompany Open Water Diver students under the indirect supervision of a PADI Instructor during:


- Swimming surface and from the entry / exit point and during exercises navigation

- When the instructor conducts a skill, such as an ascent or descent, a Divemaster can remain with other student divers (with a student individually or buddy team)

- Accompany student divers during Adventure Dives or the Specialty training dives under the indirect supervision of a PADI Instructor.

- Conduct the PADI Seal Team AquaMission

- Conduct subsequent dives for Discover Scuba Diving participants,under the indirect supervision of an instructor after they have completed successfully the first dive with a PADI Instructor.

Prerequisites

- To do this course you should:

- Be 18 years of age

- Be PADI Advanced Open Water Diver (or qualifying certification from another training organization)

- Be PADI Rescue Diver (or qualifying certification from another organization training)

- Have the Emergency First Response Primary and Secondary Care (or be training qualified in CPR and first aid from another organization training) completed within the last 24 months

- Have at least 20 dives to begin the course and 60 for certification

- Be fit for diving and submit a Medical Statement (PDF) signed by a physician within the last 12 months
