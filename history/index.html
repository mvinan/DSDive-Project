DISCOVERY

On March 10, 1535, the Spanish Bishop Tomas de Berlanga, a journey made by the Pacific, lost his way he had set, reaching mysterious islands, but saving for seafarers concerned who believed they had reached a world of enchantment. This place where they arrived was that made those Galapagos islands were given this name because of the huge turtles that inhabit since time ago.

They are also called Enchanted Islands, fantasies growing worldwide, a number of legends became more famous in these parts, converted today in the Ecuadorian province twentieth.The Galapagos archipelago is a group of islands of Ecuador, which is located in the Pacific Ocean since 1832, is considered one of the treasures of the most important natural history of the world.
The unique diversity of flora and fauna arouses the curiosity of both environmentalists and tourists.

GEOGRAFY

WE ARE FROM

The Galapagos are an oceanic archipelago, ie that there was never connections between continents and these landmasses, form an isolated group of 128 islands located in the Pacific Ocean about 1000 km west of the coast of Ecuador, and is located in the Ecuador line and extends over an area of more than 7900 km².

The islands have flat edges and a mountainous interior whose high craters are highlights, rising to 1707 meters. Several volcanoes are still active.

The Galapagos are still a region with intense volcanic activity. There have been more than 50 eruptions since the 16th century latest Las occurred in 1995 in Fernandina and Isabela in 1998.

Many theories attempt to explain the origin of the Galapagos Islands, but modern studies have shown that emerged from the seabed by successive eruptions of underwater volcanoes that rise above the Galapagos platform.

FLORA AND FAUNA

BIODIVERSITY

Galapagos has an ecosystem where flora and fauna unique in the world, fauna and flora that inhabit the Galapagos Islands are descended from settlements of small towns who managed to cross the barrier of the oceans are developed.

The biggest natural laboratory in the world, as it has been named the archipelago, has within its wildlife, giant tortoises (Galapagos), marine and land iguanas, lava lizards, sea lions, seals, albatrosses, frigate birds, boobies, cormorants, penguins , gulls, flamingos and other unique species in its genus, and six species of giant tortoises are unique in the world.

The Galapagos Islands are known for their biodiversity, especially for its high level of endemism, finding a variety of terrestrial and marine species lesser extent. On the outskirts of the land where not much rain, vegetation is very dry, but is much more lush in the highest and misty lands.

HUMAN HISTORY

The Galapagos has no indigenous human population, not least because most of the islands are lacking in fresh surface water and ground cultivable.Se think that early humans who visited the islands were Incas, led by Túpac Yupanqui in the 15th century.

The islands are uninhabited at the time when the Bishop of Panama, Tomas de Berlanga, were discovered by accident while sailing to Peru. Seeing the giant turtles that inhabited the islands, called them Galapagos. The first maps were drawn in 1574, although indigenous say that American Indians and knew before, recalling the story of Tupac Yupanqui.

Over the next three centuries, the islands were occupied by many pirates who gave them the names of their kings and captains. It is only in 1892 when the Ecuadorian government gave them their present names. The whalers also often cast anchor in the islands, and carried on their turtles vessels used as reservoirs of fresh meat, which almost decimated the local species. So this region was used as a supply base for long.

CHARLES DARWIN

THE THEORY

The Galapagos Islands have one of the most important environmental milestones in the history of the environment, they found evidence for Darwin's theory of evolution, probably the scientific theory that has changed the world view. Darwin at that time was a young man who had undertaken an exploratory trip, travel and above all the experience and the collection of specimens of animals of the Galapagos Islands led to the development and crystallization of a set of ideas that would lead to the theory of developments for 5 weeks.

Darwin fascinated by geographical isolation and distribution of species. On the voyage on the Beagle, published in 1845, he documented the natural history epic. His speech on the theory of evolution was not published until 1859, when the first edition of On the Origin of Species emerged in England and changed the study of evolutionary biology. In 1959 the Galapagos National Park was declared to protect more than 97% of the land area of the islands and thus restrict the establishment of settlements in the archipelago. In 1964 the advice and international cooperation was sought, through an agreement signed with the Charles Darwin Foundation, which established the Charles Darwin Research Station and set Scientific inventories and actions for the conservation of the archipelago.

International recognition of the exceptional value of the Archipelago, in 1979 the Heritage Committee of the United Nations-UNESCO designated the Galapagos Islands as a World Heritage Site; and later in 1985 also declared the Archipelago Biosphere Reserve.

WEATHER

The Galapagos comprise two stations, which are determined by the variable presence of the Humboldt Current, which is an ocean current that brings fresh water from the south. This stream cools and dries the climate of the Galapagos. This trend is the first season, which lasts from July to October.

When these currents weaken, warmer, typical of tropical climates, water coming from the north and usher in the hot, rainy season during the rest of the year.

Temperature has a particular behavior, since for every 100 m of elevation decreases by 1 ° C. Likewise, rainfall varies from 350-1800 mm, at altitudes ranging from 0 to 700 meters above sea level. The heat decreases in late April, when a period of cooler weather and not so cold starts.
